📖 How to Read This Dashboard

A beginner's guide to understanding your stock data

📊 Understanding the Price Change

▲ Stock is UP

"The stock is up $6.46 (+2.43%) today. If you owned 10 shares, you'd be $64.60 richer today — without doing a thing!"

▼ Stock is DOWN

"The stock is down $3.20 (−1.1%) today. If you owned 10 shares, your portfolio dipped $32.00 — but remember, daily dips are completely normal!"

🔢 What Each Number Means

Metric In Simple Words ↑ Goes Up means... ↓ Goes Down means...
P/E Ratio How many rupees/dollars you pay for every ₹1 of the company's profit. It's the market's opinion of the company's future. 😊 Investors are more optimistic and willing to pay a premium for the stock. 🤔 The stock may be getting cheaper — could be a buying opportunity or a warning sign.
EPS How much profit the company earned for each share you own. Think of it as your share of the company's paycheck. 🎉 The company is earning more money — great sign of a healthy, growing business! ⚠️ The company's profits are shrinking. Worth watching closely if it keeps falling.
Beta How "wild" or stable the stock's price movements are compared to the overall market. Beta of 1 = moves exactly with the market. 🎢 The stock swings more than the market — higher potential reward, but also higher risk. 😌 The stock is becoming more stable and predictable. Lower risk, smoother ride.
Div Yield The annual cash payment you receive just for holding the stock, shown as a percentage of its price. Free money for loyal holders! 💰 You earn more passive income per share — great for long-term, income-focused investors. 📉 The passive income is shrinking. Could mean the company is keeping profits to reinvest in growth.
52W High/Low The highest and lowest price the stock has touched over the past year — its trading "ceiling" and "floor." 🚀 Stock is near its yearly best — strong performance, but check if it has more room to grow. 🛒 Stock is near its yearly worst — could be a discount buying opportunity, or a trend downward.
Mkt Cap The total value of the entire company in the stock market right now. Large cap = huge company; small cap = smaller company. 📈 The company is becoming more valuable — investors believe in its future! 📉 The company's total value is shrinking — worth investigating why.

🧭 Reading the Sentiment Meter

🟢 Bullish / Positive

Most news articles and social posts are optimistic about this stock. The crowd thinks it will go up.

🟡 Neutral

Opinions are mixed — some good news, some bad. A good time to do more research before deciding.

🔴 Bearish / Negative

Most news is pessimistic. The market crowd is cautious. This doesn't always mean "sell" — but pay attention!
